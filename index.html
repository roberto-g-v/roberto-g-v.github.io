Roberto Gonzalez Vallejo

When I was little my father bought one of the newly release Compaq computer with Windows 2.0 that had MS-DOS and I can remember really clearly what a mesmerizing feeling of joy it was. I immediately started to play with it and since then I have a close relationship with all kinds of data and computers. In middle-school my IT teacher showed me how to code webpages and have the best memories of it, plus grades couldn’t be better. Moreover, after high-school when it came to choose my profession I couldn’t decide between a “Computer Science Degree” or a “Hospitality Degree”. I ended up choosing Hospitality because my father owns hotels and restaurants. However in college I was really good at subjects like statistics, computing programing and advance excel.

Looking foward to a promising future

Around the world human beings are creating a symbiosis that deepens the connection between individuals and computers in a very fast pace. We already have a mobile device and internet that allow us to connect within seconds. As an MBA candidate 2020 on “Data Analytics I have the perfect opportunity to study all the data inside the environment of computer science and analyze how it affects as an end product to society.

Joining the dots

Looking back at my life, now I can join the dots that brought me until this very precise moment of shifting my attention and career into something that I love “Data Analytics”. Looking forward I see a bright future with rapid technology shifts holding our progress. As my favorite writer Isaac Asimov once said “If my doctor told me I had only six minutes to live, I wouldn't brood. I'd type a little faster.” I would say to any employer reading this - let’s begin typing, coding and programming faster than Speedy Gonzalez.
